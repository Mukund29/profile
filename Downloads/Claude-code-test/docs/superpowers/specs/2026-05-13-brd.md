## 1. Document Purpose and Scope

This Business Requirements Document (BRD) defines the business context, stakeholder needs, regulatory obligations, revenue model, and high-level system requirements for **FinTrack** — a cross-platform personal finance tracking mobile application targeting users in India, the United States, and the United Kingdom.

This document serves as the contract between business stakeholders and the product/engineering team. It is the upstream input for the Product Requirements Document (PRD).

---

## 2. Business Context

### 2.1 Business Problem
The personal finance management (PFM) market is dominated by tools that are either:
- **Too complex** (YNAB at $14.99/mo, steep learning curve)
- **Too passive** (bank apps that show transactions but offer no budget intelligence)
- **Geographically limited** (Mint/Copilot US-only; no app serves India + US + UK well)
- **Abandoned by users** (90% of manual-entry budgeting apps are abandoned within 30 days)

There is a clear white space for a **simple, automated, affordable, global** PFM app that works across India's UPI/SMS ecosystem, US Plaid ecosystem, and UK Open Banking — priced accessibly at $2.99/month.

### 2.2 Business Opportunity
- **India:** 150M+ financially active smartphone users with no dominant automated PFM app
- **US:** $14.99 YNAB gap — strong demand for a cheaper, simpler alternative
- **UK:** Post-Monzo generation expects Open Banking integration as baseline

### 2.3 Strategic Objectives
| Objective | KPI | Year 1 Target |
|-----------|-----|--------------|
| Build a profitable subscription product | MRR | $2,990/mo (1,000 users) |
| Achieve product-market fit | NPS | ≥ 45 |
| Establish trust as the India-first global PFM | App Store rating | ≥ 4.4 stars |
| Prove unit economics | LTV:CAC ratio | ≥ 3:1 |
| Minimise churn through habit loop | Annual churn | ≤ 25% |

## 4. Business Requirements

### 4.1 BR-001 — Subscription Revenue Model
**Requirement:** The application shall generate revenue exclusively via a subscription model.

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Price | $2.99/month billed annually ($35.88/year) |
| Billing | Apple App Store (iOS) and Google Play Store (Android) via RevenueCat |
| Cancellation | User can cancel anytime; access continues until period end |
| Grace period | 7 days after renewal failure before feature gate |
| JWT exposure | Maximum 60-minute access post-cancellation (JWT-embedded entitlement) |
| Free trial | 14-day free trial for new subscribers |
| Refund policy | Per App Store / Play Store policy (30 days) |

**Rationale:** Subscription model aligns revenue with ongoing value delivery. $2.99/mo is a proven price point ($35.88/year) that sits below the psychological $3/month threshold while sustaining the infrastructure cost (~$71/mo at 1,000 users = 2.5% of revenue).

### 4.2 BR-002 — Geographic Market Coverage
**Requirement:** The application shall support users in India, the United States, and the United Kingdom at launch.

| Region | Bank Integration | Currency | Regulatory Framework |
|--------|----------------|----------|---------------------|
| India | Setu AA + SMS parsing (Android) | INR | RBI data localisation |
| United States | Plaid | USD | CCPA |
| United Kingdom | TrueLayer (PSD2) | GBP | GDPR / FCA |
| Others | Manual entry only | Any | Best-effort compliance |

### 4.3 BR-003 — Data Privacy and Security
**Requirement:** All user financial data shall be encrypted end-to-end. No raw SMS shall be stored or transmitted. Bank credentials shall never be exposed to the client application.

| Layer | Requirement |
|-------|------------|
| On-device | SMS parsed on device; only structured output (amount, merchant, date) transmitted |
| In transit | TLS 1.3 minimum on all connections; certificate pinning on mobile |
| At rest | Supabase AES-256 encryption; bank OAuth tokens in Supabase Vault |
| PII | Date of birth encrypted at rest (Vault); audit log of all access |
| Deletion | Cascade wipe on account closure (GDPR Article 17) |

**Marketing use:** These properties shall be highlighted as trust pillars in all marketing materials.

### 4.4 BR-004 — Regulatory Compliance
**Requirement:** The application shall comply with all applicable financial data regulations in each operating market.

| Regulation | Jurisdiction | Requirement | Implementation |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|----------------|
| GDPR | UK / EU | Right to access, rectification, deletion; data minimisation | Export endpoint, cascade delete, minimal PII collection |
| CCPA | California / US | Right to know, delete, opt-out of sale | Deletion flow, no data sold clause in privacy policy |
| RBI Data Localisation | India | Financial data of Indian users stored in India | Supabase ap-south-1 region for India users |
| COPPA | US | No users under 13 | DOB verification at signup, age ≥ 18 enforced server-side |
| PSD2 | UK / EU | Open banking consent, strong customer authentication | TrueLayer handles SCA; consent tracked |
| RBI Account Aggregator | India | Licensed AA framework for financial data sharing | Setu/Finvu AA integration; consent recorded |
| App Store Guidelines | Global | Financial app specific requirements | SMS rationale, Apple Sign-In, privacy nutrition label |

### 4.5 BR-005 — Platform Availability
**Requirement:** The application shall be available on both iOS (≥ 16.0) and Android (≥ 10.0) from launch.

| Platform | Minimum Version | Key Capability |
|----------|----------------|----------------|
| iOS | 16.0 | Face ID, Keychain, no SMS access |
| Android | 10.0 (API 29) | SMS_READ, fingerprint, Keystore |

### 4.6 BR-006 — Infrastructure Cost Efficiency
**Requirement:** Infrastructure costs shall not exceed 10% of monthly recurring revenue at any user scale up to 50,000 users.

| Scale | MRR | Max Infra Cost | Actual Estimate |
|-------|-----|---------------|-----------------|
| 1,000 users | $2,990 | $299 | ~$71 (2.5%) |
| 5,000 users | $14,950 | $1,495 | ~$220 (1.5%) |
| 10,000 users | $29,900 | $2,990 | ~$380 (1.3%) |

### 4.7 BR-007 — Transaction Auto-Capture
**Requirement:** The system shall automatically capture ≥ 60% of user transactions without manual entry for users with at least one connected bank account.

| Channel | Platform | Coverage |
|---------|----------|---------|
| SMS parsing | Android | Major Indian banks (HDFC, ICICI, SBI, Axis, Kotak, Yes Bank, PNB) |
| Email parsing (Gmail/Outlook) | iOS + Android | International bank email alerts |
| Plaid webhook sync | iOS + Android | 12,000+ US/CA institutions |
| TrueLayer webhook sync | iOS + Android | 50+ UK institutions |
| Setu AA pull (4h) | iOS + Android | 100+ Indian FIPs |

### 4.8 BR-008 — Financial Report Export
**Requirement:** Users shall be able to export weekly, monthly, and annual financial reports in Excel format and save directly to Google Drive.

| Export Type | Format | Delivery |
|------------|--------|---------|
| Weekly report | .xlsx (Excel) | On-device download or Google Drive |
| Monthly report | .xlsx (Excel) | On-device download or Google Drive |
| Annual report | .xlsx (Excel) | On-device download or Google Drive |
| Auto-export | .xlsx (Excel) | Google Drive (optional, weekly/monthly toggle) |
| Sheet structure | Setup, Daily Tracker, Weekly Analysis, Monthly Analysis, Yearly Calendar, Calc | Mirrors original Excel template |

## 5. Business Rules

| Rule ID | Rule | Rationale |
|---------|------|-----------|
| BRL-001 | Budget percentages (Needs/Wants/Savings) must sum to exactly 100% | Mathematical integrity |
| BRL-002 | Transactions are tracked from bank connection date only; no historical import | Simplicity, avoids data overload |
| BRL-003 | Balance is refreshed automatically once per day (00:30 UTC); manual refresh rate-limited to 1 per 15 minutes per connection | Cost control (Plaid API) |
| BRL-004 | First calendar month where `tracking_from` is mid-month is marked as partial and excluded from annual discipline score | Statistical accuracy |
| BRL-005 | All monetary storage uses smallest currency unit (paise/cents/pence); no floating-point arithmetic | Financial precision |
| BRL-006 | FX rate used for base-currency conversion is locked at transaction insert time | Historical accuracy |
| BRL-007 | User must be ≥ 18 years old (server-validated via DOB) to create an account | Financial services regulatory requirement |
| BRL-008 | Premium features are gated server-side via JWT custom claims; not client-side only | Security — prevents bypass |
| BRL-009 | Raw SMS text is never transmitted to or stored on servers; only parsed output is sent | Privacy regulation + marketing trust pillar |
| BRL-010 | Google Drive auto-export folder must be user-selected; no default creation in root | User data ownership |

## 6. Non-Functional Business Requirements

### 6.1 Availability
- **Uptime SLA:** 99.5% monthly for all core APIs
- **Planned maintenance:** < 2 hours/month, scheduled during lowest-traffic window (02:00–04:00 UTC)
- **Bank sync uptime:** Balance cache ensures app remains functional even during provider outages

### 6.2 Performance
| Operation | Target Response Time |
|-----------|---------------------|
| App launch to dashboard | < 2 seconds |
| Balance display (cached) | < 200ms |
| Manual balance refresh | < 5 seconds |
| Transaction list load | < 1 second |
| Report generation | < 3 seconds |
| Excel export generation | < 8 seconds |

### 6.3 Scalability
- Architecture supports 50,000 concurrent users without structural changes
- Database partitioned at 10M transaction rows
- Python parser horizontally scalable on Railway

### 6.4 Security
- Penetration test required before public launch
- OWASP Mobile Top 10 checklist completed pre-submission
- Dependency vulnerability scan (npm audit, pip-audit) in CI/CD pipeline
- No hardcoded secrets in codebase; all secrets via environment variables / Supabase Vault

### 6.5 Accessibility
- WCAG 2.1 Level AA compliance
- Dynamic font size support (iOS Dynamic Type, Android font scaling)
- VoiceOver (iOS) and TalkBack (Android) compatible
- Minimum touch target: 44×44pt

## 7. Revenue Model and Financial Projections

### 7.1 Revenue Model
```
Revenue = Active Subscribers × $2.99/month

Active Subscribers = New Subscribers - Churned Subscribers
Churn Rate Target = ≤ 2.1%/month (25% annual)
```

### 7.2 Three-Year Financial Projection

| Year | Users (EOY) | ARR | Infra Cost | Gross Margin |
|------|------------|-----|-----------|--------------|
| Year 1 | 1,000 | $35,880 | ~$852/yr | ~97.6% |
| Year 2 | 5,000 | $179,400 | ~$2,640/yr | ~98.5% |
| Year 3 | 15,000 | $538,200 | ~$6,600/yr | ~98.8% |

*Note: Gross margin = (ARR - Infra) / ARR. Does not include salaries, marketing, or App Store 30% commission.*

**App Store commission impact:**
- Apple / Google take 30% in year 1, 15% in year 2+ (small business program)
- Net revenue per subscriber after commission: Year 1 = $2.09/mo, Year 2+ = $2.54/mo

### 7.3 Unit Economics
| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| ARPU | $35.88/year |
| Target LTV (3-year, 25% annual churn) | ~$75 |
| Target CAC | ≤ $25 (LTV:CAC = 3:1) |
| Payback period | ≤ 8.5 months |
